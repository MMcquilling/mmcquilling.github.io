Honors Thesis

University of Texas at Austin

My thesis considers Agatha Christie's detective novels, not as a new phenomenon, but rather as an extention of timeless mystery traditions such as those explored by Ann Radcliffe, 1764-1823, and Walter Scott, 1771-1832.

Although seperated by time, place, and genre, these authors share an interest in reading experiences and embrace a stylistic blend of the sublime and the kitschy, which, as I argue, are fundamental to detective fiction.

Armchair Investigations; Or, The Sublime and the Kitschy: Dimensions of Literary Experience in the Mystery Fiction of Ann Radcliffe, Walter Scott, and Agatha Christie
